So far, our strategy to combat rising sea levels has been to build some walls and elevate structures - an expensive plan. Miami raised a $400mm bond back in 2017 to raise roads. Cost estimates to protect current infrastructure through seawalls and drainage projects are in the billions per municipality. Even NYC, the wealthiest city by GDP, would struggle to afford the $120B seagate proposed in one study.

In addition to many other climate change mitigation strategies, the Oceanix team is addressing this challenge and reframing this conflict by designing, building, and operating floating real estate. Their vision is to create cities - buildings, parks, plumbing, power, security, and other infrastructure needs - for a vibrant extension of coastal cities into the ocean. We anticipate the prototype by 2025, with the first complete floating community by 2035.

Liz Stein Systems Engineer & Partner

Liz Stein is Systems Engineer and Partner at Prime Movers Lab. While her background is in Aerospace Engineering, she’ll be providing in-depth technical diligence more broadly to the energy, transportation, infrastructure, and manufacturing investment verticals. She evaluates solutions from a systems perspective and considers how various elements integrate together to make a comprehensive product.

Liz’s 15 years of engineering experience runs the spectrum of extreme environments from near absolute zero cold to blazing hot (-400°F liquid hydrogen turbopumps to +3500°F hypersonic vehicles). Liz enjoys working on challenging problems in places that encourage innovative thinking. She spent the beginning of her career in a small R&D environment working on rocket propulsion technologies and clean energy combustors, taking designs from concept through demonstration testing. She has spent the last 6 years of her career on hypersonics capability development at Lawrence Livermore National Lab, focusing her final two years on strategic planning and management of the Flight Sciences team. One of her primary initiatives was engaging with external hypersonics R&D activities and starting collaborations with NASA, SNL, AFRL, industry, and university partners. Liz remains engaged professionally through AIAA, serving as the HyTASP technical committee Communications chair. Liz received her Masters and Bachelors both in Aerospace Engineering, from Georgia Institute of Technology and University of Florida, respectively.

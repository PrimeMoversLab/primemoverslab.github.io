Two things stand out on this chart. SpaceX, in orange boxes, is the only company to have achieved an order of magnitude reduction in launch costs in the last 40 years. All other medium- to heavy-launch providers thus far have maintained a price between $10,000-$20,000 per kg to LEO. (The outlier in 1981 is the first reusable launch vehicle: the Space Shuttle.) With the Falcon Heavy, SpaceX has dropped launch costs under $2,000 per kg. This demonstrated history of success in lowering costs makes SpaceX the de facto standard for the industry.

SPEAKERS

FOUNDING & BUILDING BREAKTHROUGH SCIENCE STARTUPS

George Church

Co-Founder at Colossal

George identifies as a sentientist who is a naturalistic worldview that grants moral consideration to all sentient beings.

Church is the recognized leader in genomics, pioneering several advances and breakthroughs that have progressed the entire field of genomics. He is the co-founder of Colossal. Additionally, Church is the Robert Winthrop Professor of Genetics at Harvard Medical School and heads the Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard University, and is a professor at Harvard and MIT.

Kam Ghaffarian

Founder and CEO at IBX, Co-Founder and Executive Chairman of X-energy, Intuitive Machines, and Axiom Space

Favorite question to ask: “What’s Your Passion?”. This simple question has led to the creation of multiple ambitious companies.

Kam Ghaffarian is a visionary entrepreneur and engineer whose mission to advance the state of humanity, and human knowledge has led him to create companies transforming the space and energy sectors. Kam has created organizations transforming the space and energy sectors. In addition to co-founding Axiom Space, his other companies include, Intuitive Machines, a leader in deliver science technology NASA’s Commercial Lunar Payload Services program, X-energy, which is developing nuclear power solutions for both terrestrial energy and space propulsion use, and IBX, an innovation and investment firm.

Jane Poynter

Founder, Co-CEO, and CXO at Space Perspective

Jane and her husband and business partner Taber MacCallum have been dubbed ‘Masters of the stratosphere,’ by Bloomberg Businessweek.

Jane is a serial space entrepreneur, author, and speaker.  Co-Founder and Co-CEO of multiple innovative space companies. Jane and her husband and business partner Taber MacCallum have been dubbed ‘Masters of the stratosphere,’ by Bloomberg Businessweek. Jane Poynter is one of only eight people to live for two years in Biosphere 2, a closed ecological system designed to advance possibilities for maintaining human life in outer space.

Jonathan Sporn

CEO & Chairman of the Board of Directors at Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals

Listed among Psychedelic Invest’s 2021 ranking of 100 Most Influential People in Psychedelics.

Dr. Sporn is a Board Certified Psychiatrist educated and trained at Duke, Univ of Miami, Tufts, The Boston Psychoanalytic Institute, and Harvard University. After practicing psychiatry in Boston and doing research at Mass General Hospital, Dr. Sporn worked at the National Institutes of Health (NIMH) where he was Chief of the OCD Clinic and helped to start up the Mood and Anxiety Program, which progressed research on ketamine and glutamate in depression. Dr. Sporn left the NIMH for Johnson and Johnson where he worked on drug development in psychiatry and neurology. He then moved to Pfizer where he worked on the development of Lyrica and was the Clinical Disease Area Expert in Neuroscience focused on Psychiatry and Addictions and the Neuroscience External R&D Leader before exiting Pfizer to Found the Biotech company, Perception Neuroscience, which is developing arketamine in neuropsychiatry. He has subsequently left Perception Neuroscience to Co-Found another Biotech company, Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals, that is a collaboration with Columbia University developing novel psychedelic-inspired therapies as the next generation of therapies.

INVESTOR PERSPECTIVES ON INVESTING IN BREAKTHROUGH SCIENCE

Gwen Cheni

Partner at IndieBio

Investing across bio and life sciences: genomics, precision oncology, synbio, future of food, biomaterials, AI/ML, therapeutics, etc. Computer + bio nerd during the week. Health enthusiast on the weekends. Former tech M&A hedge fund portfolio manager turned biotech venture capitalist.

Gwen is a Partner at IndieBio NY, advising and investing in companies at all stages of growth. Even before officially joining SOSV, Gwen was an active investor in IndieBio companies, including Tinctorium Bio/Huue, Lupa Bio/Intrinsic Medicine, and SyntheX. Gwen was a Venture Partner at Fusion Fund, an early stage fund in Palo Alto focused on AI/ML, robotics, and medical devices, and a Director at Studio X, a machine learning venture studio based in Austin. Gwen spent 3 years with Singularity University Ventures, an impact-focused accelerator, and helped launch their first batch of startups in the Nordic campus. Prior to venture, Gwen spent 18 years on Wall Street investing in public equities, started her career at Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and promoted to portfolio manager at a $300 million innovation-themed hedge fund.

Lindy Fishburne

Managing Partner at Breakout Ventures and Executive Director at Breakout Labs

Radical science lover, building a bio-based future.

Lindy Fishburne is founder of Breakout Labs, the seed-stage fund for hard science at the Thiel Foundation, and managing partner at Breakout Ventures. She began working with Peter Thiel in 2007 after a career in management consulting, advising Fortune 500 companies on profitable growth strategies. Fishburne has a deep understanding of what it takes to make it through the early days in business, having managed two startups through rapid growth and eventual sale. She created Breakout Labs' novel revolving fund to accelerate innovation and redefine how early-stage hard-science companies are funded and the role philanthropy can play. She launched Breakout Ventures in 2016 to facilitate investments in Breakout Labs companies gaining market traction and ready for traditional venture investment. She holds a bachelor's degree from Duke University and an MBA from the University of Texas, Austin.

Swaroop Kittu Kolluri

Founder & Managing Director at Neotribe Ventures

Founder NeotribeVC, entrepreneur, sports nut (Lakers, 49ers, cricket), golf, photography, music, yoga, poker, biking, IIT, politics, Pinot Noir, watches, fasting.

Kittu is a Silicon Valley veteran with a career spanning three decades as entrepreneur, CEO, senior operating executive and venture capitalist. Career highlights include working as an engineer at Silicon Graphics, co-founding Healtheon/WebMD, being founder and CEO of Neoteris, and investing in over four dozen successful companies as a general partner at NEA. Kittu is also a serious cricket lover—The Hyderabad Sunrisers are his favorite team. In February of 2017, Kittu founded NeoTribe Ventures with the vision of helping innovative, early stage technology startups realize their full potential. Prior, Kittu joined NEA in 2006 as a General Partner, where he served for 11 years focusing on information technology and energy technology investments.

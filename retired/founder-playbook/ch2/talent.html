Talent

How to Run

OK, you just signed a term sheet for your financing! Contrary to popular belief, this is the time to put your foot on the gas, not let up to catch your breath. The best founders drive the process after signing the term sheet to rapidly close, here is how.

First, establish a clear timeline and project plan with your lead investor and your counsel for all steps until the closing date. This will typically be four to six weeks and look something like one week for document drafting, one week for legal diligence, one week for iteration on the documents, and one week for document review from follow-on investors. This gives a few weeks of flex time for things to go wrong. Get clear alignment from the lead investor on the exact timing for each of these steps. Anything you leave to chance will take longer than expected.

Second, connect your lawyers to the lead investor’s lawyers so that they can begin working together. Set clear expectations with your lawyers about what they can and can’t do without your permission and make sure they know the timeline you are working to. Direct them to make everything as vanilla, template and simple as possible in order to avoid extra delays or costs.

Third, ensure you get a clear checklist of legal diligence items from your lead investors that your counsel can deliver to their lawyers.

Fourth, get to work on filling out your round with folks you already spoke to and referrals from the lead investor. Give a clear timeline and sense of urgency in order to shake-out who is serious about participating now.

Good luck closing out your round and hope this is helpful!
